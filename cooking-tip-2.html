How To Cook A Perfect Steak Every Time

This is the best way to cook a steak, without a doubt. And if that didn't get you... It takes little to no active time, is all done in a pan, and is extremely easy as long as you follow some of these really simple rules. Plus, with Valentines day coming around the corner, and also because steak is the best, this seemed like a good idea to upload. So whether you're cooking a ribeye, t-bone, sirloin, or strip steak, this recipe is for you.

Step Description
1 Choose a high-quality steak with marbling and a thick cut.
2 Remove the steak from the refrigerator and let it come to room temperature for 30-60 minutes.
3 Preheat your oven to 400°F (204°C).
4 Season the steak generously with salt and black pepper on both sides.
5 Heat a cast-iron skillet on high heat until it's smoking hot.
6 Add a high smoke point oil such as canola or grapeseed oil to the skillet.
7 Add the steak to the skillet and sear for 2-3 minutes on each side until a brown crust forms.
8 Add butter, garlic, and herbs to the skillet, and baste the steak with the melted butter using a spoon.
9 Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and roast for 6-8 minutes for medium-rare, or until the desired internal temperature is reached.
10 Remove the skillet from the oven and let the steak rest for 5-10 minutes to allow the juices to redistribute.
